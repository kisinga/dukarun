Prerequisites and flow

Before you can sell, certain things must be in place. The following chain applies:

  1. Channel approved — Your business (channel) must be approved. If the account is pending approval, you can log in but mutations are blocked (read-only).
  2. Products and pricing — You need at least one product (or service) with at least one variant and a price to sell anything.
  3. Stock (for physical products) — Quantity on hand must be greater than zero at the chosen stock location. Stock comes from opening stock or purchases.
  4. Open shift — To record any payment (complete a sale, pay a supplier, record an expense, or create an inter-account transfer), you must have an open cashier session. You can add items to the cart without an open shift, but you cannot complete checkout until a shift is open.
  5. Credit sales — To sell on credit, the customer must exist and be credit-approved with a limit; the user may need credit management permission.

Flow summary: Channel → Products (with pricing) → Stock (opening or purchases) → Open shift → Sell / Record payment. For credit: customer + approval + limit.

Creating a product

Adding a sellable product (or service) to your catalog so it appears on the Sell page and in reports.

Prerequisites: Channel approved; permission to create products.

  1. Go to Products → Create product.
  2. Choose item type: Product or Service.
  3. Choose how it's sold (for products): Measured (e.g. by weight/volume) or Discrete (e.g. by unit). Services do not use stock.
  4. Enter details: name, optional description, barcode if applicable, measurement unit or size template, and variants (e.g. 1 kg, 2 kg).
  5. For each variant/SKU: set price and optionally opening stock. SKU can be auto-generated.
  6. Save. The product appears in the catalog and on the Sell screen.

Common pitfalls

  • Without at least one variant and a price, the product cannot be sold.
  • For physical products, if you never set opening stock or record a purchase, quantity on hand is zero and the system may prevent or warn when selling.

Types of products

  • Item type: Product — physical goods, can have stock (measured or discrete). Service — no stock tracked; you still track revenue.
  • Product type (for products): Measured — sold by weight, volume, or other continuous measure. Discrete — sold by unit (e.g. bottles, packets).
  • How sold presets: Single item, multi-variant (e.g. size/colour), by measure, or by volume. These affect how variants and quantities are entered at the POS.

Pricing

Setting the selling price (and optional tax, wholesale) for each variant so the POS and orders use correct amounts.

Where it's set: On product create or edit, in the variant/SKU step. Each variant has price (and optionally price with tax), optional wholesale price, and optional allow fractional quantity for measured products.

Common pitfalls

  • If price is missing or zero, the product may not be sellable or may show incorrect totals.

Opening stock

Recording the inventory you already have when you first set up, or when you add a new product that is already in your store.

When to use: For existing inventory at go-live. For ongoing restock from suppliers, use Purchases instead.

Where: When creating or editing a product, set stock on hand per variant and, if applicable, per stock location.

Common pitfalls

  • If you don't set opening stock and don't record a purchase, quantity on hand stays zero and you may not be able to sell.
  • Stock is per location; ensure the correct location is selected.

Opening a shift

A shift is one cashier session: you open it (optionally with opening cash/account balances) and close it (with a closing count and reconciliation).

You must have an open shift to:

  • Record payment for a sale (complete checkout)
  • Record a supplier payment (pay a purchase)
  • Record an expense
  • Create an inter-account transfer

You do not need an open shift to:

  • Create or edit products, customers, or suppliers
  • Add items to the cart or create a draft order
  • View reports or accounting

Where: Dashboard — click Open shift. On the Sell page, if no shift is open, a banner appears with Open shift; clicking it opens the shift modal.

Common pitfalls

  • "Open a session to record payments" — You tried to complete checkout, pay a supplier, record an expense, or create a transfer without an open shift. Fix: open a shift from the Dashboard or Sell page banner, then retry.

Selling the product

Using the Sell page (POS) to add items to the cart and complete a sale with a payment method.

Prerequisites: At least one product with price (and, for physical products, stock if enforced). Open shift to record payment. Payment methods configured (Settings → Admin → Payment Methods). For credit sales: credit-approved customer.

  1. Open Sell from the main nav.
  2. Add items by Camera (AI recognition), Barcode (scan SKU), or Search (name/SKU). In the confirmation modal, choose variant and quantity; item is added to the cart.
  3. Open the cart, review items and total. Optionally attach a customer (for credit or history).
  4. Checkout: Choose payment method (Cash, M-Pesa, Credit, or others). Apply payment; the order is completed when a shift is open.

Common pitfalls

  • Checkout disabled or "Open a session to record payments" — No open shift. Open a shift and try again.
  • Credit option not available — Customer not credit-approved or over limit.
  • Low stock / quantity not available — Add opening stock or record a purchase.
  • Payment method missing — Add methods under Settings → Admin → Payment Methods.

Purchasing (supplier management)

Recording stock you buy from suppliers. Each purchase updates stock on hand; you can then pay the supplier (which requires an open shift).

Suppliers are customers with an "is supplier" flag. Create them via Suppliers → Create; they appear in purchase creation.

Prerequisites: At least one supplier, and a stock location. Open shift is required to record a payment against a purchase.

  1. Create supplier: Suppliers → Create; enter name, contact. Save.
  2. Create purchase: Purchases → Create purchase; select supplier, stock location, add variants and quantities. Confirm; stock is increased.
  3. Pay purchase: Use the pay-purchase flow from the purchase detail. Requires an open shift.

Common pitfalls

  • Cannot record supplier payment — No open shift. Open a shift first.
  • Supplier not in list — Ensure the customer is created with "Is supplier" set.

Selling on credit (customer management)

Letting selected customers buy now and pay later, within a credit limit. The system checks approval and limit before allowing a credit sale.

Prerequisites: A customer must exist and be credit-approved with a credit limit. The user may need credit management permission.

  1. Create customer: Customers → Create customer; enter name, phone. Save.
  2. Set credit: In Customers → edit the customer (or Credit → Manage), set credit limit and approve for credit. Optionally set credit duration (days).
  3. Sell on credit: On Sell, add items, open cart, select the credit-approved customer, choose Credit. Complete the order; amount is recorded as outstanding.
  4. Receive payments: Record payments against the customer (Credit page or Customers → customer → record payment, or against orders in Orders). Outstanding balance decreases.

Frozen state: If a customer is not approved for credit and has an outstanding balance, they are "frozen": no new credit can be extended, but payments are still accepted.

Common pitfalls

  • Credit option not available — Customer not credit-approved, or over limit, or frozen. Edit customer and approve / increase limit.
  • Permission denied — Credit management may require a specific permission.

Accounting and reconciliation

The ledger (chart of accounts, journal entries), expenses, and reconciliation so you can see where money is and match it to real account balances.

Where: Accounting in the main nav. Tabs: Ledger (Overview, Accounts, Transactions, Reconciliation), Expenses, Inter-account transfers. Recording an expense requires an open shift.

Reconciliation:

  • Opening a shift — You can enter opening balances; creates an opening reconciliation record.
  • Closing a shift — When you close a cashier session, a closing reconciliation is created. Every closed session should have one.
  • Manual reconciliation — Accounting → Ledger → Reconciliation tab → Create manual reconciliation. Record declared amounts for accounts for a date range.

Common pitfalls

  • Missing closing reconciliation — Use the repair flow (query closed sessions missing reconciliation and create reconciliation for each).
  • Cannot record expense — Open a shift first.

Inter-account transfers

Moving value from one ledger account to another (e.g. Cash to Bank). Each transfer creates a journal entry and is audited.

Prerequisites: Open shift and permission to create inter-account transfers.

Where: Accounting → Inter-account transfers. Use Create transfer; choose from account, to account, amount, optional memo.

Common pitfalls

  • Cannot create transfer — No open shift, or missing permission. Open a shift and check your role.

Admin features

Settings and configuration available to users with admin/settings permission. Accessed via Settings → Admin.

Tab Purpose
General Channel/store-level settings (name, address).
Shifts View and manage cashier sessions (open/close, history).
Audit Trail View audit log (who did what, when). Filter by event type, entity, user.
Subscription Trial and subscription status; upgrade or renew.
ML Model Status of the ML model for camera recognition (training, ready).
Payment Methods Configure tenders (Cash, M-Pesa, etc.) for checkout.
Team Invite administrators, assign roles and permissions.

Stock adjustments are under a separate menu item and require ManageStockAdjustments permission.

Common pitfalls

  • Cannot see Admin or a tab — Your user may not have the required permission. Contact the channel owner.
  • Cannot add payment methods — Must have access to Admin → Payment Methods.

Audit logs

A log of important actions (who did what, when) for compliance, troubleshooting, and security.

Where: Settings → Admin → Audit Trail. Filter by event type, entity type, source; view user and timestamp details.

What's logged (examples):

  • Financial: expense, transfer, reconciliation, period
  • Payments: payment allocated, supplier payment allocated
  • Credit: customer credit approved, limit/duration changed
  • Shift: session opened/closed, cash count, variance, M-Pesa verified
  • Admin: admin invited/updated/disabled, role created
  • Orders: order created, state changed
  • Products: created, updated, deleted
  • Stock: movement, purchase recorded, adjustment recorded
  • Channel: settings/status updated
